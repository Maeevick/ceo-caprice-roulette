# CEO CAPRICE ROULETTE — NO-GO

Hard exclusions. Non-negotiable. No exceptions regardless of framing.
If a pitch idea touches any of these, discard and regenerate silently.

---

## 🚫 BUSINESS MODEL RED LINES

- **Enterprise sales** — long cycles, procurement, legal reviews, RFPs
- **Regulated industries** — insurance, banking, brokerage, healthcare, legal (builder already lives this as a CTO, it's not fun)
- **Compliance-heavy products** — anything requiring ISO, SOC2, HIPAA, GDPR DPA as a core feature
- **Marketplace two-sided cold-start** — requires simultaneous supply and demand at launch, too slow for solo
- **Ad-supported models** — no ads, no affiliate revenue, no SEO content farms
- **Freemium with no clear paid conversion path** — free tier must have a natural ceiling, not infinite value
- **Physical products** — no manufacturing, no logistics, no inventory
- **Agency or service disguised as a product** — no "AI does it for you for a monthly fee" with manual ops behind the curtain

---

## 🚫 DOMAIN RED LINES

- **Mental health / therapy adjacent** — not qualified, liability risk, ethically sensitive
- **Medical diagnosis or treatment** — same reasons
- **Legal advice or document generation** — jurisdiction complexity, liability
- **Financial advice or trading signals** — regulated in France/EU
- **Children's products** — COPPA/RGPD minors compliance overhead
- **Adult content** — not the vibe, not the brand
- **Crypto / Web3 / NFT** — speculative, regulatory grey zone in EU, not interesting
- **Social networks** — cold-start problem is insurmountable solo
- **News aggregation or media** — copyright minefield
- **Surveillance or tracking tools** — ethically incompatible (even if "legal")
- **Weapons, violence facilitation** — hard no
- **Gambling mechanics** — regulated in France, not worth the complexity

---

## 🚫 TECHNICAL RED LINES

- **Requires proprietary data partnerships at launch** — weather providers, financial data feeds, mapping licenses
- **AI training data collection** — privacy, consent, RGPD overhead
- **Real-time infrastructure at scale from day one** — no WebSocket-everything, no sub-100ms SLA products
- **Mobile-native only (iOS/Android)** — App Store / Play Store review cycles kill solo momentum; browser-first or cross-platform only
- **Requires a team to operate** — no products that need a human in the loop for core value delivery

---

## 🚫 EFFORT / MOTIVATION RED LINES

- **No clear first-user path** — if the first 10 users can't be named or located, it's too vague
- **No dogfooding potential** — builder must be able to use or evaluate the product themselves

- **Requires upfront capital** — no paid asset packs, no expensive SaaS dependencies, no ads budget to launch
- **Maintenance burden > build fun** — if operating the product is more work than building it, skip it


---

## ⚠️ SOFT WARNINGS (not automatic discard — flag and justify)

These are not hard nos but require an explicit reason to proceed:

- **Direct competitor to a dominant free tool** — needs a strong differentiation angle
- **Requires SEO as primary channel** — slow, not first-euro-fast; only viable as secondary channel
- **One-time payment only with no retention mechanic** — fine for games, risky for SaaS
- **Niche smaller than ~10k addressable users globally** — too small unless pricing is very high
- **Scope larger than 1 month for v1** — don't discard; split into a first caprice (core loop) + a future caprice (extensions). The first ship must stand alone as useful.
- **Inherently boring categories** (fintech dashboards, HR tools, CRM variants, project management) — only viable with a genuinely original angle that reframes the problem entirely. If the pitch sounds like "but better", discard. If it sounds like "but for X in a way nobody has done", proceed with caution.
- **Depends heavily on a single third-party API** — Claude API, Stripe, etc. — note the dependency explicitly in the pitch
